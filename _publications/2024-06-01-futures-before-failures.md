Surgical AI is no longer a promise on the horizon. Platforms combining robotic assistance, mixed reality navigation, and real-time clinical decision support are entering operating rooms. Yet the values embedded in these systems, the choices about whose data they train on, whose bodies they optimise for, and whose autonomy they quietly override, are rarely deliberated before deployment or revisited as systems evolve. We present an anticipatory approach to fairness evaluation in surgical AI. Through stakeholder deliberation, thematic analysis, and design fictions, we derive VIRTUES, a ten-value framework for evaluating future surgical AI. We then follow a single fictional surgical AI platform, SAGE (the Surgical Assistance and Guidance Engine), whose component capabilities are individually documented today, across three future scenarios and evaluate its trajectory using structured valence coding. Our central finding is that the most consequential design decisions are neither clearly beneficial nor harmful, but Mixed, simultaneously advancing some values while eroding others. Left unresolved, these tensions accumulate until they become the structural conditions of care. By making such tensions visible before deployment, VIRTUES supports anticipatory fairness evaluation throughout the evolution of surgical AI.
